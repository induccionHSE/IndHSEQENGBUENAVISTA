7. HIGH-RISK TASKS

These tasks are considered critical due to their potential to cause serious or fatal incidents if not managed with adequate controls. The following are minimum initial requirements; however, additional controls may be required based on specific risk assessment.

Regulatory Framework Reference: Decree 1072 of 2015, Resolution 0312 of 2019, and specific regulations by task type.

ğŸ§—â€â™‚ï¸ Work at Heights

Requires formal authorization and rigorous compliance with technical standards. Applies to tasks performed from 2 meters (approx 6.5 ft) or more above the lower level.

  • Valid work at heights training certificate.
  • Use of certified harness, lifelines, and fall arrest systems.
  • Completed and signed work permit.
  • Inspection of the area, equipment, and anchor points.

Regulation: Res 4272 (2021).

ğŸš§ Confined Spaces

Applies to tanks, ducts, chambers, or other enclosed structures with limited access. Strict protocols must be followed before entry.

  • Prior evaluation and authorization by competent personnel.
  • Gas measurement, mechanical ventilation, and continuous monitoring.
  • Active rescue plan and permanent external surveillance (Watchman).
  • Specific permit and work area signage.

Regulation: Res 0491 (2020).

ğŸ”¥ Hot Work

Includes activities generating heat, sparks, or open flame (welding, cutting, grinding, etc.).

  • Authorized work permit and atmosphere analysis if applicable.
  • Presence of fire extinguishers and fire control materials.
  • Removal of nearby flammable materials.
  • Surveillance (Fire Watch) during and after activity.

Regulation: NFPA 51B.

ğŸ—ï¸ Lifting Operations

Requires technical planning and supervision. Applies to the use of cranes, hoists, booms, or lifting maneuvers.

  • Technical inspection of slings, hooks, and accessories.
  • Evaluation of the environment and lifting equipment capacity.
  • Presence of a certified signalman (rigger) with direct line of sight.
  • Demarcated zone with no personnel under the load.

Regulation: ASME B30 Series / NTC 2769.

âš ï¸ Hazardous Energies (LOTO)

Covers tasks involving contact with electrical, mechanical, hydraulic, pneumatic, or other energy sources.

  • Prior identification of all energized sources.
  • Effective application of the LOTO (Lockout/Tagout) system.
  • Verification of complete isolation before intervening.
  • Specific training and personnel authorization.

Regulation: OSHA 1910.147.

ğŸ“Œ Also considered high-risk tasks: deep excavations, structural demolition, mechanical drilling, entry into classified areas, among others. Always consult the internal procedure and coordinate with the HSEQ area.
